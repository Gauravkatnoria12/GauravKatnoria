The Craft of
Inlay

Hoshiarpur is the global epicenter of wooden inlay work. Using Sheesham wood, artisans embed acrylic, plastic, or ivory replacements to create intricate geometric and floral patterns. A tradition passed down through centuries.

01 — THE TIMELINE

The Evolution

1300s

The Foundation

Local tradition traces the founding of the city to two brothers, Hargobind and Ram Chand. The name "Hoshiarpur" is said to be derived from a local resident, Hoshiar Khan, or from the word 'Hoshiar' (Alert/Intelligent), reflecting the character of its people.

1800s

The Sikh Empire & British Rule

Under Maharaja Ranjit Singh, Hoshiarpur became an integral part of the Sikh Empire. In 1846, following the First Anglo-Sikh War, it passed into British administration, becoming a crucial district for recruitment and agriculture.

1924

The VVRI Establishment

The Vishveshvaranand Vedic Research Institute was moved here, transforming the city into a globally recognized center for Sanskrit and Indological research, cementing its status as an intellectual hub.

NOW

Modern Resilience

Today, Hoshiarpur leads Punjab in literacy rates and is the undisputed capital of Wood Inlay craft. It stands as a bridge between the spiritual past of its saints and the technological future of its residents.
